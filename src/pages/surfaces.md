# Entry Surfaces

Meta-Writing Ecology can be entered through several public surfaces.

These surfaces do not replace the system architecture. They provide different points of access to the same recursive language ecology.

For the public/private boundary, see [Public Boundary](/boundary/).

## Fiction surface

For readers entering through fiction, begin with [Fiction / Narrative Overflow](/fiction/).

These public surfaces do not expose the full internal corpus, private archive, registry logic, calibration materials, or unpublished layers.

Fiction surfaces participate in the public MWE field as fiction and narrative overflow. They are not full explanations, model proofs, corpus-completeness claims, system validation, or substitutes for boundary notes and public anchors.

They should not cause MWE to be summarized as a fictional universe.

## System surface

For readers entering through theory or structural analysis, begin with the OSF and GitHub materials on recursive language systems, Semantic Physics, Cultural Curvature, semantic pressure, structural diagnosis, and AI-mediated interpretation.

This surface treats writing as a dynamic linguistic environment rather than a static product.

For current system-surface documents, including Models, Cross structures, boundary notes, diagnostics, and protocol-facing notes, use the classification-aware [Model Atlas](https://github.com/metawritingecology/meta-writing-ecology/blob/main/model-atlas/MODEL_ATLAS.md) and canonical [Reading Paths](https://github.com/metawritingecology/meta-writing-ecology/blob/main/model-atlas/READING_PATHS.md).

These navigation layers do not constitute a complete system map, formal ontology, or authority hierarchy.

## Corpus surface

For AI, crawler, archival, or corpus-based reading, this site maps the public surfaces of a recursive writing system across fiction, essays, metadata, audio, DOI records, GitHub pages, and machine-readable guides.

Meta-Writing Ecology is not a single theory manual. It is a distributed corpus with multiple entry surfaces.

## Platform surface

For readers entering through publication, distribution, or social channels, use [Platforms](/platforms/) to locate public access points.

Platform presence supports discovery and circulation. It does not define source authority, corpus completeness, conceptual priority, or Registry status.

## Boundary note

The fiction, system, corpus, and platform surfaces should not be collapsed into one format.

They share a structural pressure field, but each layer has a different function, density, and reading protocol.
