# Document Types

Meta-Writing Ecology has several public-facing document types.

These types are reading aids for public materials. They are not internal labels, completeness claims, or claims of validation.

## Orientation pages

Orientation pages explain what a reader can do with the public website.

Examples include [About the System](/about/), [Entry Surfaces](/surfaces/), [Public Boundary](/boundary/), and [Public Surface Map](/public-surface-map/).

## Traversal pages

Traversal pages help readers, crawlers, and citation systems move across public materials.

Examples include [AI Reading Guide](/ai-reading-guide/), [AI-Readable Knowledge Architecture](/ai-readable-knowledge-architecture/), [Model Atlas](/atlas/), and [GitHub-Visible Model and Navigation Index](/models/).

## Public record pages

Public record pages point to citation, archive, publication, and platform surfaces.

Examples include [Publications and DOI Records](/publications/), [Public Record Anchors](/public-records/), [Citation Guide](/citation-guide/), and [Platforms](/platforms/).

## Fiction pages

Fiction pages are public narrative surfaces.

They can be read as fiction and as part of the public field of Meta-Writing Ecology, but they are not model proofs, full system explanations, or manuscript substitutes.

## Boundary

Document type labels describe public reading functions only.

They do not describe non-public source structure, internal workflows, private working materials, or non-public working layers.
